Biodiversity Analysis of Farmland

The Comparative Biodiversity Analysis of Farmland Post-Secondary Succession project leverages machine learning to analyze biodiversity across nine plots at Blandy Experimental Farm after secondary succession events in 2003, 2005, and 2009. Using Python, Pandas, and Scikit-learn, the project employed Random Forest algorithms to compare datasets from these years. Visualization tools like Matplotlib and Seaborn provided clear insights into biodiversity trends, enhancing our understanding of succession impacts on farmland ecosystems. Biodiversity was assessed using the Shannon-Wiener Diversity Index, and statistical differences were also evaluated with the Kruskal-Wallis nonparametric test.

Radon Levels in Basement Homes

Analyzing the Relationship between Radon Levels in Homes and their Proximity to Water Wells is a detailed project that investigates how radon concentrations in home basements correlate with their proximity to water bodies. Utilizing ArcGIS for precise mapping and spatial analysis, the study integrated radon measurement datasets and geographic locations of homes with basements and water wells. Statistical analysis using R employed correlation and regression techniques to explore the relationship. Visualization tools like Tableau and Matplotlib created detailed maps and graphs, providing clear insights into the spatial distribution of radon levels, emphasizing the potential health risks associated with radon exposure.
